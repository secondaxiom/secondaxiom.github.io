Construction of the Real Numbers

The real numbers can be "built" by extending outwards from the natural numbers, through the integers, rational numbers, and, finally, the irrational numbers. In this way, the reals inherit familiar properties and behavior from the naturals that we would want and expect them to have.

The Natural Numbers

We begin by defining the natural numbers. The natural numbers are the set \( N \) of all numbers that satisfy the following five axioms:

Peano Axioms.

  1. 0 is a natural number;
  2. Closure. If \(x\) is a natural number, then there is a unique number \(S(x)\), called the successor of \(x\), that is also a natural number;
  3. \(S(x) \neq 0\);
  4. If \(S(x) = S(y)\), then \(x = y\);
  5. Axiom of Induction. If some property \(P\) holds for \(x\), and \(P\) also holds for \(S(x)\), then \(P\) holds for all \(x\) in \(N\).
